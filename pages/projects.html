Breakout DDQN

This study presents an in-depth analysis of a Double Deep Q-Network (DDQN) applied to the Atari game Breakout. The focus is on evaluating the DDQN's learning efficiency and decision-making process compared to a standard Deep Q-Network (DQN).

Through a series of experiments, we explore the DDQN's ability to master the game, emphasizing its enhanced learning strategy over conventional DQN approaches.

The findings demonstrate the DDQN's superior performance in terms of faster convergence and higher scores, offering valuable insights for advancing reinforcement learning techniques in complex environments.

Conway's Game of Life

Experience the fascinating world of cellular automata with this interactive Game of Life Simulator, crafted using the p5.js library. This digital representation brings to life Conway's famous Game of Life, where cells evolve on a grid based on simple rules.

Users can actively participate in the simulation, creating or erasing life by clicking or dragging across the canvas. The setup includes adjustable parameters like simulation speed, providing a hands-on experience in this virtual ecosystem.

Combining educational insights with engaging interactivity, this project is a wonderful exploration of algorithmic beauty and complexity arising from simple rules, inviting both enthusiasts and curious minds to delve into the world of cellular automata.
